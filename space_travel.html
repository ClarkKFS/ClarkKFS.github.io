Space Travel Advice

What you need to take:

  • Plenty of spare underwear and socks
  • A copy of 'Space Travel for Dummies'
  • A banana so you can do the floating banana trick
  • A soft toy to help you sleep through 20 years of space travel

How to prepare for space travel:

  • Get plenty of exercise. You don't want to look like a prune when you arrive to your location
  • Time to learn the list of the 20 most popular landmarks, to make you trip more enjoyable
  • Say goodbye to those staying behind. You probably won't see them again
  • Get good insurance, because we don't have any

Space dangers you should know about:

  • Space Monkeys. They may look cute but will kill you for a banana
  • Space garbage. Don't want this getting sucked into the space thrusters
  • Leaks in your spacesuit. This can get pretty nasty pretty quickly
  • Low Gravity. Don't want to jump too high, or else you might jump off the planet

Some space adventures you can take

  • Rope Jump. Jump whilst tied to a rope and experience atmospheric pressure at its greatest as you move out of orbit
  • Virtual Earth. There may be times when you miss home, and virtually you can sniff the grass and smell the roses
  • Orbital rocks. Fly through an asteroid field and come close to losing your lunch in this truly frightening experience
  • Space Frisbee. Throw a frisbee that doesn't come back. Nothing special about this one
